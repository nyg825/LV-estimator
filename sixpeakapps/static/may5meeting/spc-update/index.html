Bonding assumption in the Extended BD plan: The new BD pipeline projects that fill FY 2028 onward have not yet been won, so we don't know whether they'll require surety bonding. The Extended BD model assumes bonding revenue and bonding costs are neutral from FY 2029 forward (i.e., they net to zero in the P&L). This presupposes that by then LV has stood up its own bonding capacity, so any bonded jobs we take on don't carry a third-party bond cost (and don't generate the small bonding revenue line either). If we instead remain reliant on third-party surety, the model will need a corresponding bonding-cost line added back.

Read of the breakeven: Without Uplifters, LV needs to land approximately $53–73M of new construction billing per year in FY28 onward to keep the consolidated business cash-positive. 05 · Strategy

Three pillars driving the next 12 months

The strategic pillars we'd like Steyn aware of and aligned on. Uplifters has its own dedicated section in this portal (Section 04) and is not duplicated here.

In motion

LV expansion complete · stabilizing the roster

The next four closings (Francis, Acama, Riverton, Lexington) wrap the LV expansion effort. Closing them stabilizes the go-forward team and locks in the operating capacity we'll build the BD pipeline against. With the roster steady, every dollar of new construction billing Tom & Chris bring in lands on a known cost base instead of triggering another hiring cycle.

BD Build-Out

Building out the BD plan · Tom & Chris leading

Sustaining $50M+/yr of new construction billing post-FY27 is the difference between the Base Case (cash burn) and Extended BD (compounding cash). Tom and Chris are leading the BD build-out, covering outbound pursuit, target list, and pipeline cadence, to put the Extended BD numbers within reach.

Active

AI-native operations at LV

LV's manual workflows for Pay Apps, Subcontractor Contracts, draws, and field reporting are the constraint on scaling the GC. The AI Mission Control program is the rebuild; current-state visuals are in Section 03.

How the three pillars connect: Wrapping the four closings (Pillar #1) stabilizes the LV roster: the PMs, supers, and back-office that the Extended BD pipeline (Pillar #2) will fill with work. The AI investment (Pillar #3) is what makes that roster capable of carrying $90–100M/yr of construction billing without another headcount step-up. The three pillars chain in order: stabilize the team, fill it with work, scale it with AI.

When the Deals Sign · Pillar #2 sequencing

The current book runs through FY27. From there, Pillar #2 (BD build-out) decides whether the line stays at the Breakeven floor (15 signings starting Oct 2028) or hits the Extended BD target (25 signings, ~bimonthly cadence starting a year earlier in Jul 2027).
